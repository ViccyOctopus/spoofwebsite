Willow Project

Destroying Alaska's Environmental Future

  • Millions of metric tons of CO2

  • Disruption of human plant
    and animal life

ConocoPhillips welcomes the Department of the Interior’s recent Record of Decision on the Willow project, an initiative that will destroy Alaska’s wilderness and the world’s hopes of climate stability. Located on Alaska’s North Slope, the Willow project is estimated to produce 9.2 million metric tons of carbon dioxide per year, a devastating blow to climate goals and protections.

While Willow-supporters argue that this project will deliver billions in revenue for the federal government and the state of Alaska, this area’s profits will take many years to discharge any significant benefits. Willow will destroy the natural environment, both with its construction and the toxins it releases. In doing so, this project will not only displace and poison native Alaskans but will also decrease the area’s biodiversity and harm already at-risk animal populations. This dangerous initiative hides behind a veil of Big Oil’s false sustainability rhetoric and ultimately, risks sacrificing the environment’s future for the riches of a few.

History of ConocoPhillips

Ranked as the 14th most polluting company in the world in 2019, ConocoPhillips boasts a troubled past in Big Oil. Having left the U.S. Climate Action Partnership, this company has been responsible for climate crisis after climate crisis and by no means will Alaska and the Willow project be an exception to this rule.

In 2011, a ConocoPhillips location was to blame for the devastating Bohai Bay oil spill. However, this incident is marred with evidence of a cover-up, where this spill only became public knowledge due to a whistleblowing blog post. ConocoPhillips has also been cited to have faulty equipment, leading to a lawsuit with the state of California over violating air pollution guidelines. Having been called the “leakiest” company in the industry, ConocoPhillips is the 13th highest ranking U.S. corporate producer of air pollution. Furthermore, this company has been involved with a plethora of human health lawsuits, where their plants have been discovered to have polluted groundwater in multiple states, poisoning locals and preventing plants from growing in the affected areas.

Additionally, those involved with ConocoPhillips have a past of political collusion and corruption, a notion that brings the passing of the Willow Project into question. This company has donated to lawmakers who refused to certify the 2020 elections, thus putting their political agenda ahead of democracy. ConocoPhillips’ attack on rights; however, does not end here as it has been found to have also donated to state officials who lobbied for anti-voter legislation. In essence, ConocoPhillips’ environmental and political history demonstrates a reputation of destruction and corruption, ideals that have backed the Willow Project and spell immense danger for the future.
